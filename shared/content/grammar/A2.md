# A2 — Elementary Spanish Grammar

## Chapter 1: The Preterite Tense (Pretérito Indefinido)

### 1.1 Regular -AR Verbs in the Preterite

The preterite (pretérito indefinido) describes completed actions in the past at a specific time.

**Endings for -AR verbs (hablar):**

| Person | Ending | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | -é | hablé |
| tú | -aste | hablaste |
| él/ella/usted | -ó | habló |
| nosotros/as | -amos | hablamos |
| vosotros/as | -asteis | hablasteis |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | -aron | hablaron |

**Key rules:**
- The nosotros form is the same as the present tense — context clarifies the meaning
- The yo and él/ella forms carry written accents

**Examples:**
- "Ayer hablé con mi madre." — Yesterday I spoke with my mother.
- "Ella trabajó ocho horas." — She worked eight hours.
- "Compramos pan esta mañana." — We bought bread this morning.

### 1.2 Regular -ER and -IR Verbs in the Preterite

-ER and -IR verbs share the same preterite endings.

**Endings (comer / vivir):**

| Person | Ending | comer | vivir |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | -í | comí | viví |
| tú | -iste | comiste | viviste |
| él/ella/usted | -ió | comió | vivió |
| nosotros/as | -imos | comimos | vivimos |
| vosotros/as | -isteis | comisteis | vivisteis |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | -ieron | comieron | vivieron |

**Examples:**
- "Comí una pizza anoche." — I ate a pizza last night.
- "¿Escribiste el correo?" — Did you write the email?
- "Vivieron en Barcelona dos años." — They lived in Barcelona for two years.

### 1.3 Irregular Preterites: Ser / Ir

"Ser" and "ir" share the same preterite forms. Context determines the meaning.

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | fui |
| tú | fuiste |
| él/ella/usted | fue |
| nosotros/as | fuimos |
| vosotros/as | fuisteis |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | fueron |

**Examples:**
- "Fui al supermercado." — I went to the supermarket. (ir)
- "Fue una fiesta increíble." — It was an incredible party. (ser)
- "Fuimos a la playa el domingo." — We went to the beach on Sunday. (ir)

### 1.4 Irregular Preterites: Estar, Tener, Hacer, Poder, Poner, Saber, Querer, Venir, Decir, Traer

These high-frequency verbs have irregular stems and a special set of unstressed endings (no accents on yo or él/ella).

**Irregular preterite endings (no accents):**

| Person | Ending |
|---|---|
| yo | -e |
| tú | -iste |
| él/ella/usted | -o |
| nosotros/as | -imos |
| vosotros/as | -isteis |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | -ieron / -eron |

**Irregular stems:**

| Infinitive | Stem | yo form |
|---|---|---|
| estar | estuv- | estuve |
| tener | tuv- | tuve |
| hacer | hic-/hiz- | hice |
| poder | pud- | pude |
| poner | pus- | puse |
| saber | sup- | supe |
| querer | quis- | quise |
| venir | vin- | vine |
| decir | dij- | dije |
| traer | traj- | traje |

**Key rules:**
- "Hacer" changes c to z in the él/ella form: "hizo" (to preserve the /s/ sound)
- "Decir" and "traer" use -eron (not -ieron) in the ellos form: dijeron, trajeron

**Examples:**
- "Estuve en casa todo el día." — I was at home all day.
- "Tuve un examen difícil." — I had a difficult exam.
- "Hizo mucho calor ayer." — It was very hot yesterday.
- "No pude dormir anoche." — I couldn't sleep last night.
- "¿Qué dijiste?" — What did you say?

### 1.5 Preterite Time Markers

| Marker | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ayer | yesterday |
| anoche | last night |
| la semana pasada | last week |
| el mes pasado | last month |
| el año pasado | last year |
| hace dos días | two days ago |
| el lunes (pasado) | (last) Monday |
| una vez | once |
| de repente | suddenly |
| en ese momento | at that moment |

**Examples:**
- "La semana pasada viajé a Madrid." — Last week I traveled to Madrid.
- "Hace tres años estudié en Londres." — Three years ago I studied in London.

## Chapter 2: The Imperfect Tense (Pretérito Imperfecto)

### 2.1 Regular -AR Verbs in the Imperfect

The imperfect describes habitual, repeated, or ongoing past actions, as well as descriptions and background in the past.

**Endings for -AR verbs (hablar):**

| Person | Ending | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | -aba | hablaba |
| tú | -abas | hablabas |
| él/ella/usted | -aba | hablaba |
| nosotros/as | -ábamos | hablábamos |
| vosotros/as | -abais | hablabais |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | -aban | hablaban |

**Key rules:**
- Only the nosotros form carries a written accent
- The yo and él/ella forms are identical — context clarifies

### 2.2 Regular -ER and -IR Verbs in the Imperfect

**Endings (comer / vivir):**

| Person | Ending | comer | vivir |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | -ía | comía | vivía |
| tú | -ías | comías | vivías |
| él/ella/usted | -ía | comía | vivía |
| nosotros/as | -íamos | comíamos | vivíamos |
| vosotros/as | -íais | comíais | vivíais |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | -ían | comían | vivían |

### 2.3 Irregular Verbs in the Imperfect

Only three verbs are irregular in the imperfect.

**Ser:**

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | era |
| tú | eras |
| él/ella/usted | era |
| nosotros/as | éramos |
| vosotros/as | erais |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | eran |

**Ir:**

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | iba |
| tú | ibas |
| él/ella/usted | iba |
| nosotros/as | íbamos |
| vosotros/as | ibais |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | iban |

**Ver:**

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | veía |
| tú | veías |
| él/ella/usted | veía |
| nosotros/as | veíamos |
| vosotros/as | veíais |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | veían |

### 2.4 Uses of the Imperfect

**Key rules:**
- Habitual past actions: "De niño, jugaba en el parque." (As a child, I used to play in the park.)
- Descriptions in the past: "La casa era grande y tenía un jardín." (The house was big and had a garden.)
- Ongoing actions: "Mientras comíamos, llovía." (While we were eating, it was raining.)
- Age, time, and weather in the past: "Tenía diez años." / "Eran las tres." / "Hacía frío."
- Physical and emotional states: "Estaba cansado y tenía hambre." (I was tired and hungry.)

**Imperfect time markers:**

| Marker | Meaning |
|---|---|
| siempre | always |
| a menudo | often |
| todos los días | every day |
| cada semana | every week |
| de niño/a | as a child |
| antes | before / in the past |
| generalmente | generally |
| normalmente | normally |
| mientras | while |
| en esa época | at that time |

**Examples:**
- "Cuando era joven, iba a la playa todos los veranos." — When I was young, I used to go to the beach every summer.
- "Mi abuela siempre cocinaba los domingos." — My grandmother always cooked on Sundays.
- "Eran las ocho y hacía mucho frío." — It was eight o'clock and it was very cold.

### 2.5 Preterite vs. Imperfect — Basic Contrast

| Preterite | Imperfect |
|---|---|
| Completed action at a specific time | Ongoing or habitual action |
| What happened | What was happening |
| Advances the story | Sets the scene / background |
| Single event | Repeated or continuous |

**Examples:**
- "Ayer llovió." — Yesterday it rained. (completed event)
- "Ayer llovía cuando salí." — Yesterday it was raining when I left. (background + event)
- "Comí a las dos." — I ate at two. (completed)
- "Siempre comía a las dos." — I always used to eat at two. (habitual)

## Chapter 3: Direct Object Pronouns

### 3.1 What Are Direct Object Pronouns?

Direct object pronouns replace the noun that directly receives the action of the verb ("What?" or "Whom?").

| Person | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| yo | me |
| tú | te |
| él / usted (masc.) | lo |
| ella / usted (fem.) | la |
| nosotros/as | nos |
| vosotros/as | os |
| ellos / ustedes (masc.) | los |
| ellas / ustedes (fem.) | las |

### 3.2 Placement Rules

**Key rules:**
- Before a conjugated verb: "Lo veo." (I see it/him.)
- Attached to the end of an infinitive: "Quiero verlo." (I want to see it.)
- Attached to the end of a gerund: "Estoy comiéndola." (I am eating it.)
- Attached to the end of an affirmative command: "Cómelo." (Eat it.)
- Before a negative command: "No lo comas." (Don't eat it.)
- When there is a conjugated verb + infinitive/gerund, the pronoun can go before the conjugated verb instead: "Lo quiero ver." / "La estoy comiendo."

**Examples:**
- "¿Tienes el libro?" — "Sí, lo tengo." — Yes, I have it.
- "¿Ves a María?" — "Sí, la veo." — Yes, I see her.
- "¿Compraste las manzanas?" — "Sí, las compré." — Yes, I bought them.
- "Voy a llamar a Pedro." — "Voy a llamarlo." / "Lo voy a llamar." — I'm going to call him.

### 3.3 Personal "a" with Direct Objects

When the direct object is a specific person (or personified entity), use the preposition "a" before it.

**Key rules:**
- "Veo a María." — I see Maria. (specific person = personal a)
- "Veo la película." — I see the movie. (thing = no personal a)
- "Busco a mi hermano." — I'm looking for my brother.
- "Busco un taxi." — I'm looking for a taxi.
- Also used with pets: "Quiero a mi perro." — I love my dog.

## Chapter 4: Indirect Object Pronouns

### 4.1 What Are Indirect Object Pronouns?

Indirect object pronouns indicate to whom or for whom the action is done ("To whom?" / "For whom?").

| Person | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| yo | me |
| tú | te |
| él/ella/usted | le |
| nosotros/as | nos |
| vosotros/as | os |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | les |

### 4.2 Uses and Placement

Indirect object pronouns follow the same placement rules as direct object pronouns.

**Key rules:**
- A clarifying phrase with "a" is often added: "Le doy el libro a María."
- Redundancy is normal and required in many cases: "A mí me gusta." / "A ella le duele."
- Indirect objects are very common with verbs like dar, decir, preguntar, enviar, escribir, comprar, regalar, enseñar, explicar, pedir, contar

**Examples:**
- "Le escribo una carta a mi abuela." — I write a letter to my grandmother.
- "¿Me puedes dar un vaso de agua?" — Can you give me a glass of water?
- "Les expliqué el problema." — I explained the problem to them.
- "Te compré un regalo." — I bought you a gift.
- "Nos enseñó la ciudad." — He showed us the city.

### 4.3 Combining Direct and Indirect Pronouns (Introduction)

When both appear together, the indirect object pronoun comes first.

**Key rules:**
- me/te/nos/os + lo/la/los/las: "Me lo dio." (He gave it to me.)
- When le/les is followed by lo/la/los/las, le/les changes to "se": "Le di el libro." → "Se lo di." (I gave it to him/her.)

**Examples:**
- "¿Me prestas tu bolígrafo?" — "Te lo presto." — I'll lend it to you.
- "Le di las flores a Ana." → "Se las di." — I gave them to her.
- "¿Nos traes el café?" — "Os lo traigo ahora." — I'll bring it to you now.

## Chapter 5: The Imperative (Commands)

### 5.1 Affirmative Tú Commands — Regular

Affirmative tú commands use the él/ella form of the present indicative.

| Infinitive | Tú Command | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| hablar | habla | speak |
| comer | come | eat |
| escribir | escribe | write |
| abrir | abre | open |
| leer | lee | read |

**Examples:**
- "Habla más despacio, por favor." — Speak more slowly, please.
- "Come tu verdura." — Eat your vegetables.
- "Abre la ventana." — Open the window.

### 5.2 Affirmative Tú Commands — Irregular

Eight common verbs have irregular affirmative tú commands.

| Infinitive | Command | Mnemonic |
|---|---|---|
| decir | di | |
| hacer | haz | |
| ir | ve | |
| poner | pon | |
| salir | sal | |
| ser | sé | |
| tener | ten | |
| venir | ven | |

**Examples:**
- "Haz la tarea." — Do the homework.
- "Ven aquí." — Come here.
- "Sal de la habitación." — Leave the room.
- "Pon la mesa." — Set the table.
- "Di la verdad." — Tell the truth.

### 5.3 Negative Tú Commands

Negative tú commands use the present subjunctive tú form.

**Formation:** No + present subjunctive (tú form)

| Infinitive | Negative Command |
|---|---|
| hablar | no hables |
| comer | no comas |
| escribir | no escribas |
| ir | no vayas |
| hacer | no hagas |
| decir | no digas |
| ser | no seas |
| tener | no tengas |

**Examples:**
- "No hables con la boca llena." — Don't talk with your mouth full.
- "No comas tan rápido." — Don't eat so fast.
- "No vayas solo." — Don't go alone.
- "No seas malo." — Don't be bad.

### 5.4 Usted / Ustedes Commands

Formal commands (usted/ustedes) always use the present subjunctive, for both affirmative and negative.

| Infinitive | Usted | Ustedes |
|---|---|---|
| hablar | hable / no hable | hablen / no hablen |
| comer | coma / no coma | coman / no coman |
| escribir | escriba / no escriba | escriban / no escriban |
| ir | vaya / no vaya | vayan / no vayan |
| tener | tenga / no tenga | tengan / no tengan |

**Examples:**
- "Pase, por favor." — Come in, please.
- "No se preocupe." — Don't worry.
- "Siéntense, por favor." — Sit down, please.

### 5.5 Pronouns with Commands

**Key rules:**
- Affirmative commands: pronouns attach to the end — "Dime." (Tell me.) / "Cómelo." (Eat it.)
- Negative commands: pronouns go before the verb — "No me digas." (Don't tell me.) / "No lo comas." (Don't eat it.)
- When attaching pronouns, add a written accent to maintain the original stress: "Diga" → "Dígame" / "Come" → "Cómelo"

**Examples:**
- "Dáselo a tu hermano." — Give it to your brother.
- "No se lo digas a nadie." — Don't tell it to anyone.
- "Escríbeme cuando llegues." — Write to me when you arrive.

## Chapter 6: Comparatives and Superlatives

### 6.1 Comparatives of Inequality

**Structure:**
- más + adjective/noun/adverb + que = more ... than
- menos + adjective/noun/adverb + que = less ... than

**Examples:**
- "Madrid es más grande que Sevilla." — Madrid is bigger than Seville.
- "Este libro es menos interesante que el otro." — This book is less interesting than the other one.
- "Tengo más amigos que tú." — I have more friends than you.
- "Habla más rápido que yo." — He speaks faster than me.

### 6.2 Comparatives of Equality

**Structure:**
- tan + adjective/adverb + como = as ... as
- tanto/a/os/as + noun + como = as much/many ... as
- verb + tanto como = as much as

**Key rules:**
- "Tan" does not change form
- "Tanto" agrees with the noun: tanto dinero, tanta comida, tantos libros, tantas casas

**Examples:**
- "Soy tan alto como mi padre." — I am as tall as my father.
- "No tengo tantos libros como tú." — I don't have as many books as you.
- "Ella trabaja tanto como yo." — She works as much as I do.

### 6.3 Irregular Comparatives

| Adjective | Comparative |
|---|---|
| bueno (good) | mejor (better) |
| malo (bad) | peor (worse) |
| grande (old) | mayor (older) |
| pequeño (young) | menor (younger) |

**Key rules:**
- Never say "más bueno" for "better" — use "mejor"
- Never say "más malo" for "worse" — use "peor"
- "Más grande" / "más pequeño" refer to physical size; "mayor" / "menor" refer to age

**Examples:**
- "Mi hermano es mayor que yo." — My brother is older than me.
- "Esta película es mejor que la otra." — This movie is better than the other one.
- "Hoy me siento peor que ayer." — Today I feel worse than yesterday.

### 6.4 Superlatives

**Relative superlative:** el/la/los/las + más/menos + adjective + de

**Absolute superlative:** adjective + -ísimo/a/os/as

**Key rules:**
- "De" is used instead of "en" (unlike English "in"): "Es el más alto de la clase." (He is the tallest in the class.)
- Remove the final vowel before -ísimo: grande → grandísimo, bueno → buenísimo

**Examples:**
- "Es la ciudad más bonita de España." — It's the most beautiful city in Spain.
- "Es el restaurante más caro del barrio." — It's the most expensive restaurant in the neighborhood.
- "La comida estaba riquísima." — The food was extremely delicious.
- "Estoy cansadísimo." — I am extremely tired.

## Chapter 7: The Present Perfect (Pretérito Perfecto)

### 7.1 Formation

The present perfect is formed with the present tense of "haber" + past participle.

**Haber (present):**

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | he |
| tú | has |
| él/ella/usted | ha |
| nosotros/as | hemos |
| vosotros/as | habéis |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | han |

**Regular past participles:**
- -ar → -ado: hablar → hablado, comprar → comprado
- -er → -ido: comer → comido, beber → bebido
- -ir → -ido: vivir → vivido, salir → salido

### 7.2 Irregular Past Participles

| Infinitive | Past Participle |
|---|---|
| abrir | abierto |
| decir | dicho |
| escribir | escrito |
| hacer | hecho |
| morir | muerto |
| poner | puesto |
| resolver | resuelto |
| romper | roto |
| ver | visto |
| volver | vuelto |

### 7.3 Uses

**Key rules:**
- Actions completed in a period of time connected to the present: hoy (today), esta semana (this week), este año (this year)
- Life experiences: "¿Has estado en México?" (Have you been to Mexico?)
- Recent past with "ya" (already) and "todavía no" (not yet)
- The past participle never changes for gender or number in compound tenses

**Examples:**
- "Hoy he trabajado mucho." — Today I have worked a lot.
- "¿Has comido ya?" — Have you eaten already?
- "Todavía no he terminado." — I haven't finished yet.
- "Este año hemos viajado a Italia." — This year we have traveled to Italy.
- "Nunca he visto esa película." — I have never seen that movie.
- "¿Alguna vez has probado la paella?" — Have you ever tried paella?

### 7.4 Present Perfect vs. Preterite

In Spain, the present perfect is used for recent or same-day events; the preterite is used for completed past events. In Latin America, the preterite is more common for both.

| Spain (present perfect) | Latin America (preterite) |
|---|---|
| "Hoy he comido paella." | "Hoy comí paella." |
| "Esta mañana he ido al médico." | "Esta mañana fui al médico." |

## Chapter 8: Prepositions — Expanded

### 8.1 Por vs. Para — Core Distinction

**Por — use for:**
- Cause / reason: "Gracias por tu ayuda." (Thanks for your help.)
- Duration: "Estudié por dos horas." (I studied for two hours.)
- Exchange: "Lo compré por diez euros." (I bought it for ten euros.)
- Movement through: "Caminé por el parque." (I walked through the park.)
- Time of day (general): "Por la mañana estudio." (In the morning I study.)
- Per: "Tres veces por semana." (Three times per week.)

**Para — use for:**
- Purpose / goal: "Estudio para aprender." (I study to learn.)
- Recipient: "Este regalo es para ti." (This gift is for you.)
- Destination: "Salgo para México." (I'm leaving for Mexico.)
- Deadline: "Es para el viernes." (It's for/due Friday.)
- Opinion: "Para mí, es fácil." (For me, it's easy.)
- Comparison: "Para un niño, cocina muy bien." (For a child, he cooks very well.)

### 8.2 Common Prepositional Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| al lado de | next to |
| delante de | in front of |
| detrás de | behind |
| encima de | on top of |
| debajo de | under |
| dentro de | inside |
| fuera de | outside |
| cerca de | near |
| lejos de | far from |
| enfrente de | across from / facing |
| a la derecha de | to the right of |
| a la izquierda de | to the left of |

**Examples:**
- "El banco está al lado del supermercado." — The bank is next to the supermarket.
- "Mi casa está cerca de la estación." — My house is near the station.
- "El gato está debajo de la mesa." — The cat is under the table.

## Chapter 9: Connectors and Sentence Structure

### 9.1 Cause and Reason

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| porque | because | No fui porque estaba enfermo. |
| como (beginning of sentence) | since / as | Como llovía, me quedé en casa. |
| ya que | since / given that | Ya que estás aquí, ayúdame. |
| por eso | that's why | Estaba cansado, por eso no salí. |

### 9.2 Contrast

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| pero | but | Es caro, pero muy bueno. |
| aunque | although | Aunque llueve, voy a salir. |
| sin embargo | however | Es difícil. Sin embargo, lo intentaré. |

### 9.3 Consequence

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| por eso | that's why | No estudié, por eso suspendí. |
| así que | so | No había taxis, así que caminé. |
| entonces | then / so | No tenía hambre, entonces no comí. |

### 9.4 Sequence

| Connector | Meaning |
|---|---|
| primero | first |
| luego / después | then / afterwards |
| más tarde | later |
| por último / finalmente | finally |
| al final | in the end |

**Examples:**
- "Primero desayuné, luego fui al trabajo y finalmente cené con amigos." — First I had breakfast, then I went to work, and finally I had dinner with friends.

## Chapter 10: The Near Future and Other Verb Structures

### 10.1 Ir a + Infinitive (Review)

Used for plans, intentions, and near-future actions.

**Examples:**
- "Voy a estudiar esta noche." — I'm going to study tonight.
- "¿Qué vas a hacer mañana?" — What are you going to do tomorrow?
- "Vamos a viajar a México." — We're going to travel to Mexico.

### 10.2 Acabar de + Infinitive (Just Did)

Expresses an action that just happened.

**Structure:** acabar (present) + de + infinitive

**Examples:**
- "Acabo de llegar." — I just arrived.
- "Acabamos de comer." — We just ate.
- "Acaba de llamar tu madre." — Your mother just called.

### 10.3 Estar + Gerund (Present Progressive Review)

For actions happening right now.

**Examples:**
- "Estoy estudiando." — I am studying.
- "¿Qué estás haciendo?" — What are you doing?
- "Están jugando en el parque." — They are playing in the park.

### 10.4 Soler + Infinitive (To Usually Do)

Expresses habitual actions.

**Examples:**
- "Suelo desayunar a las ocho." — I usually have breakfast at eight.
- "¿Qué sueles hacer los fines de semana?" — What do you usually do on weekends?
- "Solíamos ir al campo de niños." — We used to go to the countryside as children. (imperfect of soler)

### 10.5 Volver a + Infinitive (To Do Again)

**Examples:**
- "Volvió a llamar." — He called again.
- "No vuelvas a llegar tarde." — Don't be late again.
- "Volví a leer el libro." — I read the book again.

### 10.6 Empezar a / Terminar de + Infinitive

**Examples:**
- "Empecé a estudiar a las tres." — I started studying at three.
- "Terminé de leer el libro." — I finished reading the book.
- "Cuando empezó a llover, entramos en casa." — When it started to rain, we went inside.

## Chapter 11: Reflexive Verbs — Expanded

### 11.1 Review of Daily Routine Reflexive Verbs

- levantarse (to get up), acostarse (to go to bed), ducharse (to shower), lavarse (to wash), vestirse (to get dressed), peinarse (to comb), despertarse (to wake up)

### 11.2 Reflexive Verbs for Emotions and States

Many verbs that express emotions, states, or changes use reflexive pronouns.

| Verb | Meaning |
|---|---|
| sentirse | to feel |
| divertirse | to have fun |
| aburrirse | to get bored |
| enfadarse / enojarse | to get angry |
| preocuparse | to worry |
| alegrarse | to be glad |
| asustarse | to get scared |
| enamorarse | to fall in love |
| cansarse | to get tired |
| equivocarse | to make a mistake |

**Examples:**
- "Me siento muy bien hoy." — I feel very good today.
- "Nos divertimos mucho en la fiesta." — We had a lot of fun at the party.
- "Se enfadó con su hermano." — He got angry with his brother.
- "Me preocupo por mi familia." — I worry about my family.
- "Se enamoró de ella en la universidad." — He fell in love with her at university.

### 11.3 Reflexive Pronouns in the Preterite and Imperfect

Reflexive pronouns follow the same placement rules in past tenses.

**Examples:**
- "Me levanté a las siete." — I got up at seven. (preterite)
- "Se duchaba todos los días." — He used to shower every day. (imperfect)
- "Nos divertimos mucho el sábado." — We had a lot of fun on Saturday. (preterite)

## Chapter 12: Describing People, Places, and Experiences

### 12.1 Physical Descriptions

**Common adjectives:**
- alto/a (tall), bajo/a (short), delgado/a (thin), gordo/a (fat), guapo/a (handsome/pretty), joven (young), viejo/a (old), rubio/a (blonde), moreno/a (dark-haired), calvo/a (bald)

**Key structures:**
- Ser for physical traits: "Es alto y moreno." (He is tall and dark-haired.)
- Tener for features: "Tiene los ojos azules." (He has blue eyes.) / "Tiene el pelo largo." (She has long hair.)

### 12.2 Character and Personality

| Adjective | Meaning | Opposite |
|---|---|---|
| simpático/a | nice, friendly | antipático/a (unpleasant) |
| amable | kind | maleducado/a (rude) |
| generoso/a | generous | tacaño/a (stingy) |
| trabajador/a | hard-working | perezoso/a / vago/a (lazy) |

| divertido/a | fun, funny | aburrido/a (boring) |
| tímido/a | shy | extrovertido/a (outgoing) |
| tranquilo/a | calm | nervioso/a (nervous) |
| responsable | responsible | irresponsable (irresponsible) |
| honesto/a | honest | mentiroso/a (dishonest) |

### 12.3 Describing Places

**Useful vocabulary:**
- grande/pequeño (big/small), bonito/feo (pretty/ugly), moderno/antiguo (modern/old), ruidoso/tranquilo (noisy/quiet), limpio/sucio (clean/dirty), seguro/peligroso (safe/dangerous)

**Examples:**
- "Mi barrio es muy tranquilo y tiene muchos parques." — My neighborhood is very quiet and has many parks.
- "La ciudad es grande y moderna, pero un poco ruidosa." — The city is big and modern, but a little noisy.

### 12.4 Describing Experiences

**Useful verbs and structures:**
- fue + adjective: "Fue increíble." (It was incredible.)
- Lo mejor fue... : "Lo mejor fue la comida." (The best part was the food.)
- Lo peor fue... : "Lo peor fue el tráfico." (The worst part was the traffic.)
- Lo pasé muy bien / mal: "Lo pasé genial." (I had a great time.)

**Examples:**
- "El viaje fue maravilloso. Lo mejor fue visitar las playas. Lo pasé muy bien." — The trip was wonderful. The best part was visiting the beaches. I had a great time.

## Chapter 14: Numbers, Quantities, and Expressions

### 14.1 Numbers 100–1,000,000

| Number | Spanish |
|---|---|
| 100 | cien / ciento |
| 200 | doscientos/as |
| 300 | trescientos/as |
| 400 | cuatrocientos/as |
| 500 | quinientos/as |
| 600 | seiscientos/as |
| 700 | setecientos/as |
| 800 | ochocientos/as |
| 900 | novecientos/as |
| 1.000 | mil |
| 2.000 | dos mil |
| 1.000.000 | un millón |

**Key rules:**
- "Cien" is used alone or before nouns: "cien personas" (100 people)
- "Ciento" is used when followed by additional numbers: "ciento cincuenta" (150)
- Hundreds agree in gender with the noun: "doscientas personas" (200 people, fem.)
- "Mil" does not change: "dos mil", "tres mil"
- "Millón" requires "de" before nouns: "un millón de euros"

### 14.2 Expressing Quantity

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| un poco de | a little of |
| mucho/a | a lot of |
| poco/a | little / not much |
| bastante | quite a lot |
| demasiado/a | too much |
| suficiente | enough |
| varios/as | several |
| algunos/as | some |
| ningún / ninguna | no / not any |

**Examples:**
- "Quiero un poco de agua." — I want a little water.
- "Hay demasiada gente." — There are too many people.
- "¿Tienes suficiente dinero?" — Do you have enough money?
