# C1 — Advanced Spanish Grammar

## Chapter 1: Advanced Subjunctive Use

### 1.1 Subjunctive in Independent Clauses

At C1, the subjunctive is not limited to subordinate clauses. It also appears in independent clauses to express wishes, doubt, or emotional reactions.

**Key constructions:**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Ojalá + present subj. | I hope (possible) | Ojalá llueva mañana. — I hope it rains tomorrow. |
| Ojalá + imperfect subj. | I wish (unlikely present) | Ojalá tuviera más tiempo. — I wish I had more time. |
| Ojalá + pluperfect subj. | I wish (impossible past) | Ojalá hubiera estudiado más. — I wish I had studied more. |
| Que + present subj. | May... / Let... | ¡Que te vaya bien! — May it go well for you! |
| Quién + imperfect subj. | If only I could... | ¡Quién pudiera volar! — If only one could fly! |
| Quién + pluperfect subj. | If only I had... | ¡Quién lo hubiera sabido! — If only I had known! |
| Así + present subj. | I hope (colloquial curse) | ¡Así le vaya mal! — I hope it goes badly for him! |

**Examples:**
- "Ojalá no haya mucho tráfico." — I hope there isn't much traffic.
- "Ojalá pudiera volver a esos tiempos." — I wish I could go back to those times.
- "¡Que tengas un buen día!" — Have a good day!
- "¡Quién pudiera estar en la playa ahora!" — If only I could be at the beach right now!

### 1.2 Subjunctive After Expressions of Probability

The choice between indicative and subjunctive with probability expressions depends on the degree of certainty.

**Indicative (high certainty):**
- Es evidente que... / Es obvio que... / Es cierto que... / Está claro que...
- "Es evidente que tiene razón." — It is evident he is right.

**Subjunctive (doubt, low certainty):**
- Es posible que... / Es probable que... / Puede que... / Es dudoso que...
- "Es posible que venga mañana." — It's possible he'll come tomorrow.
- "Puede que llueva esta tarde." — It might rain this afternoon.

**Negated certainty → subjunctive:**
- "No es cierto que sea así." — It's not true that it's like that.
- "No está claro que vayan a ganar." — It's not clear they're going to win.

### 1.3 Subjunctive in Concessive Clauses

Concessive clauses admit a contrasting point while asserting the main idea. The mood depends on whether the conceded fact is real or hypothetical.

**Aunque:**
- Indicative (fact): "Aunque está lloviendo, salgo." — Although it IS raining, I'm going out.
- Subjunctive (hypothetical): "Aunque esté lloviendo, saldré." — Even if it IS raining, I'll go out.

**Advanced concessive expressions (always + subjunctive):**

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| por más que | no matter how much | Por más que lo intente, no lo consigo. |
| por mucho que | no matter how much | Por mucho que estudie, no aprueba. |
| por poco que | no matter how little | Por poco que haga, siempre ayuda. |
| por muy + adj. + que | however (adj.) | Por muy cansado que esté, sigue trabajando. |
| aun cuando | even when / even if | Aun cuando lo sepa, no dirá nada. |
| si bien | while, even though | Si bien es difícil, no es imposible. |
| a sabiendas de que | knowing full well | Lo hizo a sabiendas de que estaba mal. |

**Examples:**
- "Por más que le expliqué el problema, no lo entendió." — No matter how much I explained the problem, he didn't understand it.
- "Por muy inteligente que sea, comete errores." — However intelligent he may be, he makes mistakes.

### 1.4 Subjunctive in Temporal Clauses — Nuances

At C1, temporal conjunctions reveal subtle shifts between indicative and subjunctive.

| Conjunction | Indicative (past/present fact) | Subjunctive (future/hypothetical) |
|---|---|---|
| cuando | Cuando llegué, llovía. | Cuando llegues, llámame. |
| en cuanto / tan pronto como | En cuanto lo vi, lo reconocí. | En cuanto lo vea, se lo diré. |
| hasta que | Esperé hasta que llegó. | Espera hasta que llegue. |
| después de que | Después de que terminó, se fue. | Después de que termine, iremos. |
| mientras | Mientras estudiaba, escuchaba música. | Mientras vivas aquí, respeta las normas. |
| antes de que | (always subjunctive) | Sal antes de que llueva. |
| una vez que | Una vez que lo supo, actuó. | Una vez que lo sepas, dime. |

**Key rule:** "Antes de que" ALWAYS takes the subjunctive, because the action in the subordinate clause hasn't happened yet by definition.

### 1.5 Subjunctive After Value Judgments

Impersonal expressions that convey a value judgment trigger the subjunctive.

| Expression | Example |
|---|---|
| Es normal que + subj. | Es normal que estés nervioso. |
| Es lógico que + subj. | Es lógico que quiera irse. |
| Es justo que + subj. | Es justo que reciban un premio. |
| Es ridículo que + subj. | Es ridículo que piense así. |
| Es una pena que + subj. | Es una pena que no puedas venir. |
| Es extraño que + subj. | Es extraño que no haya llamado. |
| Es sorprendente que + subj. | Es sorprendente que haya ganado. |
| Está bien / mal que + subj. | Está mal que no diga la verdad. |
| Más vale que + subj. | Más vale que te des prisa. |

## Chapter 2: Advanced Conditional Structures

### 2.1 Review: Three Main Types + Mixed

| Type | Si-clause | Result clause | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (real) | present indicative | future / present / imperative | possible |
| 2 (hypothetical) | imperfect subjunctive | conditional | unlikely present |
| 3 (counterfactual) | pluperfect subjunctive | conditional perfect | impossible past |
| Mixed A | pluperfect subjunctive | conditional | past cause → present result |
| Mixed B | imperfect subjunctive | conditional perfect | present cause → past result |

### 2.2 De + Infinitive as a Conditional Alternative

"De + infinitive" can replace "si + conjugated verb" in formal or literary registers.

**Structure:** De + infinitive (simple or compound), + result clause

**Key rules:**
- "De + infinitive" = si + present/imperfect subjunctive (hypothetical)
- "De + haber + past participle" = si + pluperfect subjunctive (counterfactual)
- More common in written Spanish

**Examples:**
- "De tener más tiempo, estudiaría más." = Si tuviera más tiempo... — If I had more time, I would study more.
- "De haberlo sabido, no habría venido." = Si lo hubiera sabido... — Had I known, I wouldn't have come.
- "De ser posible, me gustaría cambiar de horario." — If it were possible, I'd like to change my schedule.
- "De no llover, iremos a la playa." — If it doesn't rain, we'll go to the beach.

### 2.3 Como + Subjunctive (Conditional Warning/Threat)

"Como" + present subjunctive functions as a conditional warning or threat, equivalent to "if (you do)..."

**Examples:**
- "Como no estudies, suspenderás." — If you don't study, you'll fail.
- "Como vuelvas a llegar tarde, te despiden." — If you're late again, they'll fire you.
- "Como me entere de que mientes, se acabó." — If I find out you're lying, it's over.

### 2.4 Con + Infinitive / Con que + Subjunctive (Provided That)

**Con + infinitive:** "Con estudiar un poco más, aprobarás." — By studying a bit more, you'll pass.
**Con que + subjunctive:** "Con que vengas a las ocho, es suficiente." — As long as you come at eight, that's enough.

### 2.5 A no ser que / Excepto que / Salvo que (Unless)

All three mean "unless" and take the subjunctive.

**Examples:**
- "Iré a no ser que llueva." — I'll go unless it rains.
- "Te esperaré, salvo que me digas lo contrario." — I'll wait for you, unless you tell me otherwise.
- "Llegaremos a tiempo, excepto que haya mucho tráfico." — We'll arrive on time, unless there's a lot of traffic.

## Chapter 3: Advanced Passive and Impersonal Constructions

### 3.1 Passive Reflex (se) vs. Impersonal se — Refined Distinction

At C1, learners must distinguish precisely between these two "se" constructions.

**Passive se (pasiva refleja):**
- The thing acted upon is the grammatical subject; the verb agrees with it
- "Se venden pisos." — Apartments are sold. (pisos = subject, plural verb)
- "Se habla español." — Spanish is spoken. (español = subject, singular verb)

**Impersonal se:**
- No grammatical subject; verb is always 3rd person singular
- "Se trabaja mucho aquí." — People work a lot here.
- "Se vive bien en España." — One lives well in Spain.
- When the object is a person, use "a" + singular verb: "Se buscó a los responsables." — The responsible parties were sought.

**Common errors to avoid:**
- Incorrect: "Se buscan a los culpables." (person + plural = wrong)
- Correct: "Se busca a los culpables." (person → singular + personal a)

### 3.2 Estar + Past Participle (Resultant State) vs. Ser + Past Participle (Action)

| Construction | Focus | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ser + participle | The action itself (who/what caused it) | La ventana fue rota por el niño. — The window was broken by the boy. |
| estar + participle | The resulting state | La ventana está rota. — The window is broken. |

**Advanced examples:**
- "El informe ya está terminado." — The report is already finished. (state)
- "El informe fue terminado ayer por el equipo." — The report was finished yesterday by the team. (action)
- "Las luces están encendidas." — The lights are on. (state)
- "Las luces fueron encendidas por el portero." — The lights were turned on by the doorman. (action)

### 3.3 Other Impersonal Constructions

| Construction | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| uno/una | one (personal) | Uno nunca sabe qué puede pasar. |
| la gente | people | La gente no respeta las normas. |
| 3rd person plural (no subject) | they (unspecified) | Dicen que va a llover. — They say it's going to rain. |
| hay que + infinitive | one must | Hay que tener paciencia. |
| se debe + infinitive | one should | Se debe respetar a los demás. |
| conviene + infinitive | it is advisable | Conviene llegar temprano. |
| basta con + infinitive | it is enough to | Basta con llamar para reservar. |

## Chapter 4: Advanced Tense Use — Nuances

### 4.1 The Historical Present (Presente Histórico)

The present tense can narrate past events vividly, as if the listener were watching them happen.

**Examples:**
- "En 1492, Colón llega a América." — In 1492, Columbus arrives in America.
- "Estaba en casa tranquilo y, de repente, suena el teléfono y me dice que..." — I was at home relaxing and suddenly the phone rings and he tells me that...

### 4.2 The Future of Conjecture (Present Probability)

The simple future expresses speculation about the present.

**Examples:**
- "¿Dónde estará Juan?" — Where can Juan be? (I wonder)
- "Serán las ocho." — It must be about eight.
- "Tendrá unos cuarenta años." — He must be about forty.
- "Costará unos cien euros." — It probably costs about a hundred euros.

### 4.3 The Conditional of Conjecture (Past Probability)

The conditional expresses speculation about the past.

**Examples:**
- "Serían las tres cuando llegamos." — It must have been about three when we arrived.
- "Tendría unos veinte años cuando se casó." — She must have been about twenty when she got married.
- "Estaría enfermo, por eso no vino." — He was probably sick, that's why he didn't come.

### 4.4 The Future Perfect of Conjecture (Recent Past Probability)

The future perfect speculates about a recently completed action.

**Examples:**
- "Ya habrá llegado." — He must have arrived by now.
- "¿Qué le habrá pasado?" — What can have happened to him?
- "Se habrán perdido." — They must have gotten lost.

### 4.5 The Conditional Perfect of Conjecture (Past-before-past Probability)

The conditional perfect speculates about an action prior to another past event.

**Examples:**
- "Habrían salido antes de que empezara a llover." — They must have left before it started raining.
- "Habría terminado el trabajo antes de irse." — He must have finished the work before leaving.

### 4.6 Temporal Clauses with Compound Tenses

**Key rule:** In temporal clauses referring to the future, use the subjunctive — including compound forms.

- "Cuando hayas terminado, llámame." — When you have finished, call me. (present perfect subjunctive)
- "No salgas hasta que haya dejado de llover." — Don't go out until it has stopped raining.
- "En cuanto hayamos cenado, nos iremos." — As soon as we have had dinner, we'll leave.

## Chapter 5: Relative Clauses — Advanced

### 5.1 The Subjunctive in Relative Clauses

The mood in relative clauses depends on whether the antecedent is known or hypothetical.

**Indicative (known/existing antecedent):**
- "Tengo un amigo que habla japonés." — I have a friend who speaks Japanese. (he exists)

**Subjunctive (unknown/nonexistent/desired antecedent):**
- "Busco a alguien que hable japonés." — I'm looking for someone who speaks Japanese. (I don't know if they exist)
- "Necesito un piso que tenga tres habitaciones." — I need an apartment that has three bedrooms. (I haven't found it yet)
- "No hay nadie que sepa la respuesta." — There's no one who knows the answer.

### 5.2 El que / Quien in Generalizations

"El que" / "quien" + subjunctive creates generalizations (whoever, anyone who).

**Examples:**
- "El que quiera participar, que levante la mano." — Whoever wants to participate, raise your hand.
- "Quien no esté de acuerdo puede irse." — Whoever disagrees can leave.
- "Los que lleguen tarde no podrán entrar." — Those who arrive late won't be able to enter.

### 5.3 Lo que / Lo cual — Referring to Clauses

"Lo que" and "lo cual" refer back to an entire idea or clause.

**Key rules:**
- "Lo que" is versatile and common
- "Lo cual" is used only in non-restrictive (explanatory) clauses and is more formal

**Examples:**
- "Aprobó el examen, lo que nos alegró mucho." — He passed the exam, which made us very happy.
- "No contestó al teléfono, lo cual me preocupó." — She didn't answer the phone, which worried me.
- "Lo que necesitas es descansar." — What you need is to rest.

### 5.4 Cuyo/a/os/as — "Whose"

"Cuyo" agrees with the possessed noun, not the possessor. It is formal and more common in writing.

**Examples:**
- "El autor cuyo libro ganó el premio es chileno." — The author whose book won the prize is Chilean.
- "La empresa cuyas oficinas visitamos está en crisis." — The company whose offices we visited is in crisis.
- "Los países cuyos gobiernos invirtieron en educación prosperaron." — The countries whose governments invested in education prospered.

### 6.3 Periphrases with Past Participle

| Periphrasis | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| tener + participle | completed result (participle agrees) | Tengo hechos los deberes. |
| llevar + participle | accumulated total (participle agrees) | Llevo leídos tres capítulos. |
| dejar + participle | to leave in a state (participle agrees) | Dejé la puerta cerrada. |
| quedar + participle | to be left in a state | La cena quedó preparada. |
| dar por + participle | to consider as | Doy por terminada la reunión. |

**Key rule:** In these constructions, the past participle agrees in gender and number with the direct object.

**Examples:**
- "Tengo escritas tres cartas." — I have three letters written. (cartas = fem. pl.)
- "Llevo leídas cien páginas." — I have read a hundred pages (so far).
- "Damos por cerrado el debate." — We consider the debate closed.

## Chapter 7: Advanced Ser vs. Estar

### 7.1 Estar for Subjective/Temporary Perception vs. Ser for Objective Classification

At C1, learners master the use of estar to express a subjective reaction, surprise, or noticeable change.

**Examples:**
- "¡Qué guapa estás hoy!" — How pretty you look today! (noticeable change)
- "El niño está muy alto." — The boy is really tall (now). (perceived growth)
- "Esta sopa está muy salada." — This soup is very salty. (tasting it now, subjective)
- "La habitación está muy pequeña con todos estos muebles." — The room feels very small with all this furniture. (subjective impression)

### 7.2 Ser with Events and Locations of Events

"Ser" is used for the location of events (not objects/people).

- "La reunión es en la sala 3." — The meeting is in room 3.
- "La fiesta será en mi casa." — The party will be at my house.
- "El concierto fue en el estadio." — The concert was at the stadium.

Compare with estar for objects/people:
- "Juan está en la sala 3." — Juan is in room 3.

### 7.3 Ser/Estar + Past Participle — Complete System

| Construction | Meaning | Agreement | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| ser + participle | passive action (by agent) | agrees with subject | El libro fue escrito por Cervantes. |
| estar + participle | resulting state | agrees with subject | El libro está escrito en español. |
| tener + participle | completed action with emphasis on result | agrees with direct object | Tengo leídas tres novelas. |
| llevar + participle | accumulated result | agrees with direct object | Llevo escritas cinco páginas. |

## Chapter 8: Discourse and Text Organization

### 8.1 Advanced Connectors by Function

**Introducing a topic:**
- En cuanto a... / En lo que se refiere a... / Con respecto a... / En relación con...
- "En cuanto a la economía, la situación ha mejorado." — Regarding the economy, the situation has improved.

**Adding information:**
- Además / Asimismo / Por otra parte / Es más / Incluso / No solo... sino también
- "Es más, el informe demuestra que los resultados son positivos." — What's more, the report shows the results are positive.

**Contrasting:**
- Sin embargo / No obstante / Ahora bien / En cambio / Por el contrario / Con todo
- "Con todo, hay que reconocer que el esfuerzo ha valido la pena." — All things considered, one must recognize the effort has been worth it.

**Expressing consequence:**
- Por lo tanto / Por consiguiente / En consecuencia / De ahí que (+ subjunctive) / Así pues
- "De ahí que muchos jóvenes emigren." — Hence many young people emigrate. (subjunctive)

**Reformulating:**
- Es decir / O sea / En otras palabras / Mejor dicho / Dicho de otro modo
- "Mejor dicho, no es que no quiera, sino que no puede." — Or rather, it's not that he doesn't want to, but that he can't.

**Summarizing / Concluding:**
- En resumen / En conclusión / En definitiva / Para terminar / En pocas palabras / Al fin y al cabo
- "Al fin y al cabo, lo importante es que estamos juntos." — At the end of the day, the important thing is we're together.

### 8.2 De ahí que + Subjunctive

"De ahí que" introduces a consequence and always takes the subjunctive.

**Examples:**
- "No estudió lo suficiente; de ahí que suspendiera." — He didn't study enough; hence he failed.
- "La situación es complicada; de ahí que necesitemos más tiempo." — The situation is complicated; hence we need more time.

### 8.3 Hedging and Softening

C1 speakers soften opinions and statements for politeness and precision.

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Diría que... | I would say that... | Diría que es una buena idea. |
| Me atrevería a decir que... | I would dare to say... | Me atrevería a decir que es el mejor. |
| En mi opinión / A mi juicio | In my opinion | A mi juicio, deberíamos esperar. |
| Por lo visto / Según parece | Apparently | Por lo visto, van a cerrar la empresa. |
| Que yo sepa | As far as I know | Que yo sepa, no han cambiado nada. |
| Al parecer | Apparently | Al parecer, el director ha dimitido. |
| Cabe señalar que | It is worth noting that | Cabe señalar que los datos son incompletos. |

## Chapter 9: Nominal Clauses — Advanced Patterns

### 9.1 Verbs of Communication + Indicative/Subjunctive

The mood depends on whether the verb conveys information or influence.

**Indicative (reporting information):**
- decir, afirmar, declarar, confesar, reconocer, admitir + que + indicative
- "Dice que viene mañana." — He says he's coming tomorrow.
- "Reconoció que se había equivocado." — He admitted he had made a mistake.

**Subjunctive (exerting influence/command):**
- decir, pedir, ordenar, rogar, exigir, suplicar + que + subjunctive
- "Dice que vengas mañana." — He says (for you) to come tomorrow.
- "Le pidió que se callara." — He asked him to be quiet.

**Key contrast:**
- "Le dijo que estaba enfermo." — He told him he was sick. (information → indicative)
- "Le dijo que fuera al médico." — He told him to go to the doctor. (command → subjunctive)

### 9.2 Verbs of Thinking/Believing — Mood Alternation

| Affirmative (indicative) | Negative (subjunctive) |
|---|---|
| Creo que es verdad. | No creo que sea verdad. |
| Pienso que tiene razón. | No pienso que tenga razón. |
| Me parece que está bien. | No me parece que esté bien. |
| Estoy seguro de que vendrá. | No estoy seguro de que venga. |

**In questions (depends on expected answer):**
- "¿Crees que es verdad?" — Do you think it's true? (neutral → indicative)
- "¿No crees que sea verdad?" — Don't you think it might be true? (doubt → subjunctive)

### 9.3 El hecho de que + Indicative/Subjunctive

"El hecho de que" (the fact that) can take either mood:
- Indicative: when the speaker treats it as an established fact
- Subjunctive: when the speaker adds judgment or when the clause is new information

**Examples:**
- "El hecho de que trabaja mucho es evidente." — The fact that he works a lot is evident. (established)
- "El hecho de que trabaje mucho no significa que sea eficiente." — The fact that he works a lot doesn't mean he's efficient. (judgment)

## Chapter 10: Word Order and Emphasis

### 10.1 Topicalization (Fronting)

Moving an element to the beginning of the sentence for emphasis or topic-setting.

**Examples:**
- "Eso no lo sabía." — That, I didn't know. (fronted direct object with redundant pronoun)
- "A Juan le regalé un libro." — To Juan, I gave a book. (fronted indirect object)
- "En esa casa vivieron mis abuelos." — In that house my grandparents lived. (fronted adverbial)
- "Cansado estoy, pero no me rindo." — Tired I am, but I don't give up. (fronted predicate)

### 10.2 Cleft Sentences (Oraciones Hendidas)

Used to highlight a specific element of the sentence.

**Structure:** Ser + highlighted element + el/la/lo que/quien + rest

**Examples:**
- "Fue Juan el que lo hizo." — It was Juan who did it.
- "Es la economía lo que más me preocupa." — It's the economy that worries me most.
- "Fueron ellos quienes decidieron." — It was they who decided.
- "Es aquí donde todo empezó." — It's here where everything started.

### 10.3 Reduplication for Emphasis

Repeating a word for emphatic effect.

**Examples:**
- "Lo que es es." — It is what it is.
- "Eso sí que es verdad." — That really is true.
- "Trabajar, trabajamos mucho." — As for working, we work a lot. (infinitive fronted + conjugated form)

## Chapter 11: Advanced Uses of "Se"

### 11.1 Complete Inventory of "Se"

At C1, learners must identify all functions of "se."

| Function | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Reflexive | Se lava las manos. | He washes his (own) hands. |
| Reciprocal | Se quieren mucho. | They love each other. |
| Passive se | Se venden pisos. | Apartments are sold. |
| Impersonal se | Se vive bien aquí. | One lives well here. |
| Pronominal verb | Se arrepintió. | He regretted it. (inherent se) |
| Indirect object replacement | Se lo di. (= le lo di) | I gave it to him. |
| Aspectual / intensive se | Se comió toda la pizza. | He ate the whole pizza. (emphatic completion) |
| Dative of interest | Se me cayó el vaso. | The glass fell on me. (involuntary) |

### 11.2 Involuntary / Accidental "Se" (Se me / Se te / Se le...)

This construction removes blame from the subject, presenting the action as accidental or involuntary.

**Structure:** Se + indirect object pronoun + verb (3rd person, agrees with the thing) + subject (thing)

**Examples:**
- "Se me olvidaron las llaves." — I forgot the keys. (lit: The keys forgot themselves on me.)
- "Se le rompió el teléfono." — His phone broke. (involuntary)
- "Se nos acabó el tiempo." — We ran out of time.
- "Se te cayó el café." — You dropped the coffee.
- "Se me ha ocurrido una idea." — An idea occurred to me.

**Common verbs in this construction:** olvidar, romper, perder, caer, acabar, ocurrir, escapar, quemar, estropear

### 11.3 Aspectual / Intensive "Se" (Se comió, se bebió...)

Adding "se" to certain transitive verbs emphasizes completeness of the action.

**Examples:**
- "Comió una pizza." — He ate a pizza. (neutral)
- "Se comió toda la pizza." — He ate the whole pizza. (completeness emphasized)
- "Leyó el libro." — She read the book. (neutral)
- "Se leyó el libro entero en una noche." — She read the entire book in one night. (emphatic)
- "Bebió tres cervezas." — He drank three beers.
- "Se bebió tres cervezas de un trago." — He drank three beers in one go.

## Chapter 12: Register and Style

### 12.1 Formal vs. Informal Register

C1 speakers adjust their language depending on context.

| Feature | Informal | Formal |
|---|---|---|
| Address | tú / vosotros | usted / ustedes |
| Greetings | ¡Hola! ¿Qué tal? | Buenos días. ¿Cómo está usted? |
| Requests | ¿Me ayudas? | ¿Sería tan amable de ayudarme? |
| Expressions | la verdad es que... | cabe destacar que... |
| Connectors | o sea, bueno, pues | es decir, por consiguiente |
| Subjunctive in -ra vs. -se | -ra form dominant | -se form also appears |
| Vocabulary | curro (job), molar (to be cool) | trabajo, resultar agradable |

### 12.2 Colloquial Intensifiers and Markers

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ¡Qué va! | No way! | ¿Estás enfadado? — ¡Qué va! |
| ¡Venga! | Come on! / OK! | ¡Venga, vámonos! |
| ¡Anda! | Wow! / Come on! | ¡Anda, no lo sabía! |
| o sea | I mean / that is | O sea, no me parece bien. |
| bueno | well | Bueno, ¿qué hacemos? |
| pues | well / so | Pues no sé qué decirte. |
| es que | the thing is | Es que no tengo tiempo. |
| total que | so in the end | Total que nos quedamos en casa. |
| resulta que | it turns out that | Resulta que ya lo sabía. |

### 12.3 Euphemisms and Politeness Strategies

| Direct | Softened |
|---|---|
| No puedo. | Me resulta imposible / Me temo que no va a ser posible. |
| Estás equivocado. | Creo que habría que reconsiderar esa idea. |
| Es malo. | No es del todo satisfactorio / Deja bastante que desear. |
| Dime la verdad. | Me gustaría que fueras sincero conmigo. |
| Hazlo. | ¿Te importaría hacerlo? / ¿Serías tan amable de...? |
