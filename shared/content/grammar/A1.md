# A1 — Beginner Spanish Grammar

## Chapter 1: Alphabet, Pronunciation & Basic Phonetics

### 1.1 The Spanish Alphabet

The Spanish alphabet has 27 letters. Key differences from English: the letter "ñ", and letters like "h" (always silent), "v"/"b" (same sound), "c"/"z"/"s" (regional pronunciation variation).

**Key rules:**
- "h" is always silent: "hola" is pronounced "ola"
- "ll" and "y" sound similar (like English "y")
- "j" and "g" (before e/i) sound like a strong English "h"
- Stress falls on the second-to-last syllable by default; accents override this

**Examples:**
- "Hola, me llamo Ana." — Hello, my name is Ana.
- "La jirafa es un animal." — The giraffe is an animal.

### 1.2 Accentuation and Written Accents

Written accent marks (tildes) signal stress and distinguish homophones.

**Key rules:**
- Words ending in a vowel, -n, or -s are stressed on the second-to-last syllable
- Written accents on question/exclamation words: qué, cómo, dónde, cuándo, quién, cuánto
- Accent marks distinguish: tú (you) vs. tu (your), él (he) vs. el (the), sí (yes) vs. si (if)

**Examples:**
- "¿Cómo te llamas?" — What is your name?
- "Él tiene tu libro." — He has your book.

## Chapter 2: Nouns and Articles

### 2.1 Grammatical Gender — Masculine and Feminine Nouns

Every Spanish noun has a grammatical gender: masculine or feminine. Articles, adjectives, and pronouns must agree.

**Key rules:**
- Most nouns ending in -o are masculine: el libro (the book), el niño (the boy)
- Most nouns ending in -a are feminine: la casa (the house), la niña (the girl)
- Exceptions: el día (day), el mapa (map) — masculine despite -a ending; la mano (hand), la foto — feminine despite -o ending
- Nouns ending in -ión, -dad, -tad, -tud, -umbre are usually feminine: la nación, la ciudad
- Nouns ending in -or, -aje are usually masculine: el color, el viaje

**Examples:**
- "El perro es grande." — The dog is big.
- "La ciudad es bonita." — The city is beautiful.
- "El problema es difícil." — The problem is difficult.

### 2.2 Plural Formation

**Key rules:**
- Nouns ending in a vowel → add -s: libro → libros, casa → casas
- Nouns ending in a consonant → add -es: ciudad → ciudades, color → colores
- Nouns ending in -z → change z to c and add -es: lápiz → lápices
- Nouns ending in an accented vowel + consonant may lose the accent: nación → naciones

**Examples:**
- "Tengo dos libros." — I have two books.
- "Hay muchas ciudades bonitas." — There are many beautiful cities.

### 2.3 Definite Articles — el, la, los, las

Used when referring to something specific or already known.

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | el | la |
| Plural | los | las |

- "el" is used before feminine nouns starting with stressed a-/ha-: el agua, el hacha (but: las aguas)

**Examples:**
- "El gato duerme." — The cat sleeps.
- "La profesora habla español." — The teacher speaks Spanish.
- "Los niños juegan en el parque." — The children play in the park.
- "Las manzanas son rojas." — The apples are red.

### 2.4 Indefinite Articles — un, una, unos, unas

Used when introducing something for the first time or referring to something non-specific.

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | un | una |
| Plural | unos | unas |

- Indefinite articles are omitted after "ser" with professions: "Soy médico" (I am a doctor), not "Soy un médico"

**Examples:**
- "Tengo un perro." — I have a dog.
- "Ella es una estudiante." — She is a student.
- "Hay unos libros en la mesa." — There are some books on the table.

### 2.5 Contracted Articles — al and del

When the preposition "a" or "de" precedes the masculine singular definite article "el", they contract.

- a + el = al (never "a el")
- de + el = del (never "de el")
- No contraction with "él" (the pronoun): "Hablo de él" (I speak about him)

**Examples:**
- "Voy al mercado." — I'm going to the market.
- "Es el libro del profesor." — It's the teacher's book.

## Chapter 3: Personal Pronouns

### 3.1 Subject Pronouns

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo (I) | nosotros/nosotras (we) |
| 2nd | tú (you, informal) / usted (you, formal) | vosotros/vosotras (you all, Spain) / ustedes (you all) |
| 3rd | él (he) / ella (she) | ellos/ellas (they) |

- Subject pronouns are often omitted because the verb ending reveals the subject: "Hablo" = "I speak"
- Use them for emphasis or contrast: "Yo hablo español, pero ella habla inglés."

**Examples:**
- "Yo soy estudiante." — I am a student.
- "Ella vive en Madrid." — She lives in Madrid.
- "Nosotros hablamos español." — We speak Spanish.

### 3.2 Tú vs. Usted — Informal vs. Formal "You"

- **tú** — used with friends, family, peers, children (informal)
- **usted** (abbreviated Ud.) — used with strangers, elders, authority figures, professional contexts (formal)
- **vosotros** — used in Spain for informal plural "you"
- **ustedes** — used in Latin America for all plural "you" (formal and informal)

**Examples:**
- "¿Cómo te llamas?" — What's your name? (informal)
- "¿Cómo se llama usted?" — What's your name? (formal)

## Chapter 4: The Verb "Ser" and "Estar" (To Be)

### 4.1 Ser — Permanent / Inherent Characteristics

"Ser" expresses identity, origin, nationality, profession, material, relationships, time, and inherent qualities.

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | soy |
| tú | eres |
| él/ella/usted | es |
| nosotros | somos |
| vosotros | sois |
| ellos/ustedes | son |

**Uses:**
- Identity/name: "Soy Ana."
- Origin/nationality: "Soy de México." / "Es española."
- Profession: "Ella es médica."
- Material: "La mesa es de madera."
- Relationship: "Él es mi hermano."
- Time/date: "Son las tres." / "Hoy es lunes."
- Physical/personality traits: "Él es alto." / "Ella es simpática."

**Examples:**
- "Soy estudiante de español." — I am a Spanish student.
- "Somos de Colombia." — We are from Colombia.
- "Hoy es martes, 5 de marzo." — Today is Tuesday, March 5th.

### 4.2 Estar — Location / Temporary States

"Estar" expresses location, temporary states, conditions, and ongoing actions.

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | estoy |
| tú | estás |
| él/ella/usted | está |
| nosotros | estamos |
| vosotros | estáis |
| ellos/ustedes | están |

**Uses:**
- Location: "El banco está en la calle mayor."
- Temporary state/feeling: "Estoy cansado." / "Ella está triste."
- Ongoing action (with gerund): "Estoy estudiando."

**Examples:**
- "¿Dónde está el hotel?" — Where is the hotel?
- "Estamos en casa." — We are at home.
- "El café está frío." — The coffee is cold.

### 4.3 Ser vs. Estar — Key Contrasts

Many adjectives change meaning depending on whether they are used with ser or estar.

| Adjective | With ser | With estar |
|---|---|---|
| bueno | Es bueno. (He's a good person.) | Está bueno. (It tastes good.) |
| malo | Es malo. (He's bad/evil.) | Está malo. (He's sick.) |
| aburrido | Es aburrido. (It's boring.) | Está aburrido. (He's bored.) |
| listo | Es listo. (He's clever.) | Está listo. (He's ready.) |
| seguro | Es seguro. (It's safe.) | Está seguro. (He's sure.) |

## Chapter 5: Regular and Irregular Verbs in the Present Tense

### 5.1 Regular -AR Verbs

The most common verb group. Remove -ar and add the endings.

| | Ending |
|---|---|
| yo | -o |
| tú | -as |
| él/ella/usted | -a |
| nosotros | -amos |
| vosotros | -áis |
| ellos/ustedes | -an |

**Common verbs:** hablar (to speak), trabajar (to work), estudiar (to study), caminar (to walk), escuchar (to listen), mirar (to look), bailar (to dance), comprar (to buy)

**Examples:**
- "Yo hablo español todos los días." — I speak Spanish every day.
- "Ella trabaja en un banco." — She works in a bank.
- "Nosotros estudiamos mucho." — We study a lot.

### 5.2 Regular -ER Verbs

| | Ending |
|---|---|
| yo | -o |
| tú | -es |
| él/ella/usted | -e |
| nosotros | -emos |
| vosotros | -éis |
| ellos/ustedes | -en |

**Common verbs:** comer (to eat), beber (to drink), leer (to read), aprender (to learn), vender (to sell), correr (to run)

**Examples:**
- "Como una manzana." — I eat an apple.
- "¿Bebes café o té?" — Do you drink coffee or tea?
- "Aprendemos mucho en clase." — We learn a lot in class.

### 5.3 Regular -IR Verbs

| | Ending |
|---|---|
| yo | -o |
| tú | -es |
| él/ella/usted | -e |
| nosotros | -imos |
| vosotros | -ís |
| ellos/ustedes | -en |

**Common verbs:** vivir (to live), escribir (to write), abrir (to open), recibir (to receive), subir (to go up)

**Examples:**
- "Vivo en una ciudad grande." — I live in a big city.
- "Él escribe una carta." — He writes a letter.

### 5.4 Irregular Verb: Tener (To Have)

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | tengo |
| tú | tienes |
| él/ella/usted | tiene |
| nosotros | tenemos |
| vosotros | tenéis |
| ellos/ustedes | tienen |

**Common expressions with tener:**
- tener + age: "Tengo veinte años." (I am twenty years old.)
- tener hambre/sed — hungry/thirsty
- tener frío/calor — cold/hot
- tener miedo — afraid
- tener razón — to be right
- tener sueño — sleepy
- tener prisa — to be in a hurry

**Examples:**
- "¿Cuántos años tienes?" — How old are you?
- "Tengo hambre. ¿Y tú?" — I'm hungry. What about you?

### 5.5 Irregular Verb: Ir (To Go)

Completely irregular. Also used to form the near future.

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | voy |
| tú | vas |
| él/ella/usted | va |
| nosotros | vamos |
| vosotros | vais |
| ellos/ustedes | van |

**Examples:**
- "Voy al supermercado." — I'm going to the supermarket.
- "¿Adónde vas?" — Where are you going?
- "¡Vamos!" — Let's go!

### 5.6 Irregular Verb: Hacer (To Do / To Make)

Irregular in the yo form only in the present tense.

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | hago |
| tú | haces |
| él/ella/usted | hace |
| nosotros | hacemos |
| vosotros | hacéis |
| ellos/ustedes | hacen |

Also used for weather: "Hace frío." (It's cold.) / "Hace sol." (It's sunny.)

**Examples:**
- "Hago ejercicio por la mañana." — I exercise in the morning.
- "¿Qué haces los fines de semana?" — What do you do on weekends?

### 5.7 Irregular Verb: Dar (To Give)

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | doy |
| tú | das |
| él/ella/usted | da |
| nosotros | damos |
| vosotros | dais |
| ellos/ustedes | dan |

**Examples:**
- "Le doy el libro a la profesora." — I give the book to the teacher.
- "¿Me das tu número de teléfono?" — Can you give me your phone number?

### 5.8 Stem-Changing Verbs (e → ie)

The stem vowel "e" changes to "ie" in all forms except nosotros and vosotros (boot verbs).

**Common verbs:** querer (to want/love), entender (to understand), preferir (to prefer), empezar (to start), pensar (to think)

**Querer conjugation:**

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | quiero |
| tú | quieres |
| él/ella/usted | quiere |
| nosotros | queremos |
| vosotros | queréis |
| ellos/ustedes | quieren |

**Examples:**
- "Quiero un café, por favor." — I want a coffee, please.
- "Ella prefiere el té." — She prefers tea.
- "La clase empieza a las nueve." — The class starts at nine.

### 5.9 Stem-Changing Verbs (o → ue)

The stem vowel "o" changes to "ue" in all forms except nosotros and vosotros.

**Common verbs:** poder (to be able to/can), dormir (to sleep), volver (to return), costar (to cost), encontrar (to find), jugar (u → ue)

**Poder conjugation:**

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | puedo |
| tú | puedes |
| él/ella/usted | puede |
| nosotros | podemos |
| vosotros | podéis |
| ellos/ustedes | pueden |

**Examples:**
- "¿Puedes ayudarme?" — Can you help me?
- "No puedo dormir." — I can't sleep.
- "¿Cuánto cuesta?" — How much does it cost?

### 5.10 Stem-Changing Verbs (e → i)

The stem vowel "e" changes to "i". Only in -ir verbs.

**Common verbs:** pedir (to ask for/order), seguir (to follow/continue), repetir (to repeat), servir (to serve)

**Pedir conjugation:**

| | |
|---|---|
| yo | pido |
| tú | pides |
| él/ella/usted | pide |
| nosotros | pedimos |
| vosotros | pedís |
| ellos/ustedes | piden |

**Examples:**
- "Pido una ensalada." — I order a salad.
- "Él siempre pide la cuenta." — He always asks for the bill.

## Chapter 6: Reflexive Verbs

### 6.1 Reflexive Verbs and Daily Routines

Reflexive verbs indicate the subject performs the action on themselves. They use reflexive pronouns placed before the conjugated verb.

**Reflexive pronouns:**

| Subject | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| yo | me |
| tú | te |
| él/ella/usted | se |
| nosotros | nos |
| vosotros | os |
| ellos/ustedes | se |

**Key rule:** With body parts, use definite articles (not possessives): "Me lavo las manos" (I wash my hands), NOT "Me lavo mis manos."

**Common reflexive verbs:**
- llamarse (to be called), levantarse (to get up), acostarse (to go to bed, o→ue), despertarse (to wake up, e→ie), ducharse (to shower), lavarse (to wash oneself), vestirse (to get dressed, e→i), peinarse (to comb one's hair), sentarse (to sit down, e→ie), sentirse (to feel, e→ie)

**Examples:**
- "Me llamo Carlos." — My name is Carlos.
- "Me levanto a las siete." — I get up at seven.
- "Ella se ducha por la mañana." — She showers in the morning.
- "Nos acostamos tarde los viernes." — We go to bed late on Fridays.

## Chapter 7: Key Grammatical Structures

### 7.1 Hay — There Is / There Are

"Hay" is an impersonal form of haber used to express existence. It is invariable — the same form for singular and plural.

- Hay + indefinite article + noun: "Hay un banco cerca."
- Hay + number + noun: "Hay tres estudiantes."
- Negation: "No hay agua."

**Examples:**
- "Hay un restaurante en esta calle." — There is a restaurant on this street.
- "¿Hay hoteles baratos aquí?" — Are there cheap hotels here?
- "No hay problema." — There is no problem.

### 7.2 Near Future: Ir a + Infinitive

Used to talk about plans, intentions, and future events.

**Structure:** ir (conjugated) + a + infinitive

**Examples:**
- "Voy a estudiar esta tarde." — I'm going to study this afternoon.
- "¿Qué vas a hacer el fin de semana?" — What are you going to do this weekend?
- "Ella va a viajar a México." — She is going to travel to Mexico.
- "Van a llegar mañana." — They are going to arrive tomorrow.

### 7.3 Present Progressive: Estar + Gerund

Used to describe an action happening right now.

**Gerund formation:**
- -ar verbs → -ando: hablar → hablando
- -er/-ir verbs → -iendo: comer → comiendo, vivir → viviendo
- Irregular gerunds: leer → leyendo, ir → yendo, dormir → durmiendo

**Examples:**
- "Estoy comiendo." — I am eating.
- "¿Qué estás haciendo?" — What are you doing?
- "Ella está estudiando para el examen." — She is studying for the exam.

### 7.4 Modal Verbs with Infinitive

Modal verbs express ability, desire, or obligation. They are conjugated and followed by an infinitive.

- **poder** (can): "Puedo hablar español."
- **querer** (to want): "Quiero aprender."
- **deber** (should/must): "Debes estudiar más."
- **necesitar** (to need): "Necesito ayuda."

**Examples:**
- "¿Puedo abrir la ventana?" — Can I open the window?
- "Quiero aprender a bailar salsa." — I want to learn to dance salsa.

### 7.5 Tener que + Infinitive (Obligation)

"Tener que" + infinitive expresses obligation or necessity.

**Examples:**
- "Tengo que trabajar mañana." — I have to work tomorrow.
- "Ella tiene que estudiar más." — She has to study more.
- "Tenemos que comprar comida." — We have to buy food.

### 7.6 Gustar and Similar Verbs

"Gustar" (to like) has a reverse structure: the thing liked is the subject; the person is the indirect object.

**Structure:** (A mí/ti/él...) + me/te/le/nos/os/les + gusta/gustan + noun/infinitive

- gusta + singular noun or infinitive
- gustan + plural noun

**Related verbs:** encantar (to love), doler (to hurt), molestar (to bother), interesar (to interest)

**Examples:**
- "Me gusta el fútbol." — I like football.
- "¿Te gustan las películas de acción?" — Do you like action movies?
- "Le encanta bailar." — He/she loves to dance.
- "Me duele la cabeza." — My head hurts.

## Chapter 8: Adjectives and Adverbs

### 8.1 Descriptive Adjectives — Gender and Number Agreement

Adjectives must agree in gender and number with the noun they describe. They generally follow the noun.

- Adjectives ending in -o change: bueno/buena/buenos/buenas
- Adjectives ending in -e or consonant don't change for gender: grande, azul
- Adjectives of nationality do change: español/española

**Examples:**
- "El coche rojo." — The red car.
- "La casa roja." — The red house.
- "Un estudiante inteligente." — An intelligent student.

### 8.2 Position of Adjectives

Most adjectives follow the noun; some come before and may shorten.

- bueno → buen (before masc. sing.): "Es un buen estudiante."
- grande → gran (before any sing. noun, meaning "great"): "Es un gran hombre."
- malo → mal, primero → primer, tercero → tercer

### 8.3 Demonstrative Adjectives — Este, Ese, Aquel

Indicate distance from the speaker.

| Distance | Masc. sing. | Fem. sing. | Masc. pl. | Fem. pl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Close (this) | este | esta | estos | estas |
| Mid (that) | ese | esa | esos | esas |
| Far (that over there) | aquel | aquella | aquellos | aquellas |

**Examples:**
- "Este libro es interesante." — This book is interesting.
- "Ese restaurante es bueno." — That restaurant is good.
- "Aquella montaña es muy alta." — That mountain over there is very tall.

### 8.4 Possessive Adjectives

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| yo | mi | mis |
| tú | tu | tus |
| él/ella/usted | su | sus |
| nosotros | nuestro/nuestra | nuestros/nuestras |
| vosotros | vuestro/vuestra | vuestros/vuestras |
| ellos/ustedes | su | sus |

- mi/tu/su do NOT change for gender
- nuestro/vuestro DO change: nuestro libro / nuestra casa

**Examples:**
- "Mi casa es grande." — My house is big.
- "Nuestra profesora es simpática." — Our teacher is nice.

### 8.5 Quantifiers and Intensifiers

- mucho/a/os/as — much/many
- poco/a/os/as — little/few
- bastante/s — quite a lot
- todo/a/os/as — all/every
- cada — each (invariable)
- muy — very (before adjectives/adverbs)
- mucho — a lot (after verbs)

**Examples:**
- "Tengo mucho trabajo." — I have a lot of work.
- "Esta sopa está muy buena." — This soup is very good.
- "Me gusta mucho la música." — I like music a lot.

### 8.6 Basic Adverbs

**Time:** hoy (today), mañana (tomorrow), ayer (yesterday), ahora (now), siempre (always), nunca (never), a veces (sometimes), luego (later), pronto (soon)

**Place:** aquí (here), allí (there), cerca (nearby), lejos (far)

**Manner:** bien (well), mal (badly), despacio (slowly), rápido (quickly)

**-mente adverbs:** formed from feminine adjective + -mente: rápida → rápidamente, fácil → fácilmente

**Examples:**
- "Ella siempre llega tarde." — She always arrives late.
- "Habla muy despacio, por favor." — Please speak very slowly.

## Chapter 9: Question Formation and Negation

### 9.1 Forming Questions

Questions use inverted question marks (¿...?). Word order is often the same as statements with rising intonation. No auxiliary verb needed (unlike English "do/does").

- "¿Hablas español?" — Do you speak Spanish?
- "¿Tienes un bolígrafo?" — Do you have a pen?

### 9.2 Question Words (Interrogatives)

All question words carry a written accent mark.

| Question word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ¿Qué? | What? | ¿Qué haces? |
| ¿Quién? / ¿Quiénes? | Who? | ¿Quién es ella? |
| ¿Cuál? / ¿Cuáles? | Which? / What? | ¿Cuál es tu nombre? |
| ¿Dónde? | Where? | ¿Dónde vives? |
| ¿Adónde? | (To) where? | ¿Adónde vas? |
| ¿Cuándo? | When? | ¿Cuándo es tu cumpleaños? |
| ¿Cómo? | How? | ¿Cómo estás? |
| ¿Por qué? | Why? | ¿Por qué estudias español? |
| ¿Cuánto/a? | How much? | ¿Cuánto cuesta? |
| ¿Cuántos/as? | How many? | ¿Cuántos años tienes? |

**Key note:** Use "qué" with nouns ("¿Qué libro lees?"), use "cuál" when choosing among options ("¿Cuál es tu dirección?")

### 9.3 Negation

Place "no" directly before the verb. Double negatives are correct in Spanish.

**Negative words:**
- no — no/not
- nada — nothing
- nadie — nobody
- nunca / jamás — never
- ningún/ninguno/ninguna — none
- tampoco — neither

**Examples:**
- "No entiendo." — I don't understand.
- "No tengo nada." — I have nothing.
- "Nunca llego tarde." — I never arrive late.
- "A mí tampoco me gusta." — I don't like it either.

## Chapter 10: Prepositions, Numbers, and Time Expressions

### 10.1 Core Prepositions

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| a | to, at | Voy a Madrid. |
| de | of, from | Soy de España. |
| en | in, on, at | Vivo en una ciudad. |
| con | with | Voy con mi amigo. |
| sin | without | Café sin azúcar. |
| por | for, by, through | Gracias por todo. |
| para | for (purpose/recipient) | Este regalo es para ti. |
| sobre | on, about | El libro está sobre la mesa. |
| entre | between | Está entre la farmacia y el banco. |
| hasta | until | Hasta luego. |
| desde | from, since | Vivo aquí desde enero. |
| hacia | towards | Camina hacia el norte. |

### 10.2 Por vs. Para — Key Distinction

**Por — use for:**
- Duration: "Estudié por dos horas."
- Exchange: "Lo compré por diez euros."
- Cause/reason: "No puedo salir por el frío."
- Movement through: "Pasamos por el parque."

**Para — use for:**
- Purpose/goal: "Estudio para aprender."
- Recipient: "Este café es para usted."
- Destination: "Salgo para México mañana."
- Deadline: "Lo necesito para el lunes."

### 10.3 Numbers 0–100

| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 — cero | 10 — diez | 20 — veinte | 30 — treinta |
| 1 — uno | 11 — once | 21 — veintiuno | 40 — cuarenta |
| 2 — dos | 12 — doce | 22 — veintidós | 50 — cincuenta |
| 3 — tres | 13 — trece | 23 — veintitrés | 60 — sesenta |
| 4 — cuatro | 14 — catorce | 24 — veinticuatro | 70 — setenta |
| 5 — cinco | 15 — quince | 25 — veinticinco | 80 — ochenta |
| 6 — seis | 16 — dieciséis | 26 — veintiséis | 90 — noventa |
| 7 — siete | 17 — diecisiete | 27 — veintisiete | 100 — cien |
| 8 — ocho | 18 — dieciocho | 28 — veintiocho | |
| 9 — nueve | 19 — diecinueve | 29 — veintinueve | |

- From 31 onwards: treinta y uno, treinta y dos, etc.
- "Uno" shortens to "un" before masculine nouns: "veintiún estudiantes"
- "Cien" becomes "ciento" before additional numbers: "ciento uno" (101)

### 10.4 Ordinal Numbers (1st–10th)

| Ordinal | Masc. | Fem. |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | primero (primer) | primera |
| 2nd | segundo | segunda |
| 3rd | tercero (tercer) | tercera |

### 10.5 Telling the Time

- "¿Qué hora es?" — What time is it?
- "Es la una." — It is one o'clock. (singular)
- "Son las dos/tres/cuatro..." — It is two/three/four...
- "Son las tres y media." — Half past three.
- "Son las cuatro y cuarto." — Quarter past four.
- "Son las cinco menos cuarto." — Quarter to five.
- "de la mañana" (a.m.) / "de la tarde" (afternoon) / "de la noche" (evening)

**Examples:**
- "La clase empieza a las nueve de la mañana." — The class starts at nine in the morning.

### 10.6 Days of the Week

lunes, martes, miércoles, jueves, viernes, sábado, domingo

- Days are lowercase and masculine
- "el lunes" = on Monday (single), "los lunes" = on Mondays (habitual)

### 10.7 Months and Dates

enero, febrero, marzo, abril, mayo, junio, julio, agosto, septiembre, octubre, noviembre, diciembre

- Months are lowercase
- "Hoy es el cinco de marzo." — Today is the fifth of March.
- Use cardinal numbers except "el primero" for the 1st
